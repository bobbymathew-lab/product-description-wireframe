# 05. Specifications (Structured Reference)

## Purpose

The specifications section provides factual, scannable information for users who want precise product details.

It supports:
- Comparison-driven decision making
- Technical validation
- Reduced support and pre-purchase queries

---

## SEO Intent

- Capture specification-based and comparison queries
- Support rich results and structured data
- Prevent overloading narrative sections with technical detail

---

## Content Guidelines

**Recommended format:**
- Table or definition list
- Labelâ€“value pairs
- Consistent ordering across similar products

**Best practices:**
- Use standardized units and terminology
- Keep values concise and unambiguous
- Group related specs together where possible

**Avoid:**
- Embedding specs inside paragraphs
- Mixing benefits or marketing language with factual data
- Duplicating spec content across multiple PDPs without normalization

---

## Structural Notes

- Should be:
  - Easily collapsible on mobile
  - Accessible via jump links
- Labels should remain meaningful without surrounding context

---

## SEO & Schema Notes

- Specifications can:
  - Feed Product schema properties
  - Support comparison pages and filters
- Keep naming consistent with schema.org fields where applicable

---

## Why This Section Matters

Poorly structured specifications often:
- Increase pre-sale friction
- Lead to support queries
- Create inconsistencies across PDPs

Clear specifications improve **trust, accuracy, and scalability**.
