# 06. FAQs (Search Demand Capture)

## Purpose

The FAQ section addresses common user questions that appear before or after purchase intent.

It helps:
- Capture long-tail and question-based queries
- Reduce pre-purchase uncertainty
- Support rich results through FAQ schema

---

## SEO Intent

- Target “People Also Ask” style queries
- Consolidate informational intent on the PDP
- Prevent cannibalisation with blog content

---

## Content Guidelines

**Recommended format:**
- 5–10 FAQs per PDP
- Question as H3 or expandable accordion header
- Clear, concise answers (40–80 words)

**Best practices:**
- Use natural-language questions
- Answer directly in the first sentence
- Avoid marketing tone

**Avoid:**
- Yes/no answers without explanation
- Duplicating entire blog content
- Overlapping heavily with support documentation

---

## Structural Notes

- Ideal as:
  - Accordion UI
  - Collapsible sections on mobile
- Each FAQ should:
  - Make sense in isolation
  - Be indexable even when collapsed

---

## Example (Placeholder)

**Does this product support fast charging?**  
Yes. The product supports fast charging, allowing a full charge in approximately two hours under standard conditions.

**Is it compatible with both iOS and Android devices?**  
Yes. The product is compatible with iOS and Android devices via Bluetooth and does not require additional software.

**Can noise cancellation be turned off?**  
Yes. Active noise cancellation can be disabled, allowing users to switch between isolation and ambient listening modes.

**Is this suitable for long travel or flights?**  
Yes. With extended battery life and active noise cancellation, it is well suited for flights and long-distance travel.

---

## Schema Notes

- FAQs can be:
  - Marked up with FAQPage schema
  - Limited to high-confidence, non-ambiguous questions
- Avoid marking up promotional or subjective questions

---

## Why This Section Matters

Well-structured FAQs:
- Capture additional SERP real estate
- Reduce bounce from informational queries
- Improve PDP relevance without content bloat
